The Monarch Butterfly: Lifecycle and Migration

Stages of Metamorphisis

Egg

  1. Female butterflies lay eggs
  2. Many eggs are laid at one time
  3. Butterfly eggs are very small in size

Caterpillar

  1. Also known as larvae
  2. Caterpillars eat a lot to store up food for later on
  3. Caterpillars shed their skin 4-5 times
  4. Caterpillars start at the size of a pinhead and grow up to 2 inches long

Pupa

  1. Also known as a chrysalis
  2. Pupa are suspended in a shaded area
  3. In this stage, the larvae grow wings, legs, and eyes to transform into butterfly
Butterfly
  1. Butterflies can fly but they can not grow anymore
  2. Adult butterflies eat nectar for energy
  3. Butterflies purpose is to mate and lay eggs

    4. Four Generations in One Year

    First Generation

    March-April

    1. Baby caterpillars ( aka larva) hatch from eggs
    2. After about four days baby caterpillars fully emerge from their eggs
    3. Caterpillars spend all of their time eating milkweed
    4. Caterpillars are fully grown after about two weeks
    5. Caterpillars find a sheltered area to form a chrysalis
    6. After 10 days the caterpillars emerge, reborn as butterflies
    7. Butterflies fly away to eat nectar from flowers
    8. Butterflies find mate and lay eggs
    9. Butterflies die after 4-6 weeks

    Second Generation

    May-June

    1. Baby caterpillars hatch from eggs
    2. Caterpillars eat lots of milkweed
    3. Caterpillars form chrysalis
    4. Caterpillars transform into butterflies and fly away
    5. Butterflies mate and lay eggs, dying after 2-6 weeks

    Third Generation

    July-August

    1. Same process as second generation

    Fourth Generation

    September-October

    1. Baby caterpillars hatch from eggs
    2. Caterpillars eat lots of milkweed
    3. Caterpillars form chrysalis
    4. Caterpillars transform into butterflies and fly away
    5. Butterflies migrate to Mexico or California
    6. Butterflies go into hibernation
    7. Hibernating Monarch Butterflies emerge from hibernation
    8. Butterflies mate
    9. Butterflies fly North-East to lay eggs on Milkweed plants, dying after 6-8 months of life
